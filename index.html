~ Notes ~

The town square is the heart of the city. Once a bustling center of activity, it now stands as a somber reminder of the city's former glory. The oppressive twilight cast by the Dark Star paints the square in sickly yellow hues, casting long, eerie shadows.

Before the curse, the Town-Square was a vibrant gathering place for festivals, markets, and community events. Statues of Blackvale's founders, now cracked and worn, stand as silent witnesses to the city's decline.

The square serves as a refuge for the remaining sane citizens. Makeshift stalls and tents offer basic supplies, and the air is filled with the hushed whispers of people sharing stories of survival and despair.

The Town-Square is a symbol of hope and resilience. Despite the pervasive madness, it remains a beacon for those fighting against Baelrath's curse.

The Broken Lantern is a dimly lit tavern located on the edge of the Town-Square. Its name comes from the cracked and flickering lantern that hangs above the entrance, a fitting emblem of the city's fractured state.

Once known as "The Golden Lantern," the tavern was a lively place where merchants, travelers, and locals gathered to share stories and enjoy a pint. The curse has transformed it into a shadow of its former self, but it still serves as a crucial meeting point.

The interior is filled with worn wooden tables, a roaring hearth, and the murmur of hushed conversations. The barkeep, a grizzled veteran named Harwin, keeps the peace and offers a listening ear to those who need it.

The Broken Lantern is a place for rest and recovery. Players can find bounties to hunt monsters, partake in side work, and hear rumors that might lead to valuable rewards. The tavern is also a source of random events that can provide small, yet crucial, rewards.

Market Street is a narrow, winding road that was once the commercial lifeline of Blackvale. Now, it stands as a testament to the city's struggle against the encroaching darkness.

Before the curse, Market Street was lined with bustling shops and stalls, offering everything from exotic spices to finely crafted goods. The air was filled with the sounds of haggling merchants and cheerful customers.

The street is now a shadowy corridor, with many shops boarded up or abandoned. The remaining merchants are determined to keep their businesses running, despite the ever-present threat of madness and monsters.

Market Street represents the enduring spirit of Blackvale's citizens. Despite the dire circumstances, the market continues to provide essential goods and services.

The General Store is a small, cluttered shop located on Market Street. It offers a variety of basic supplies that adventurers need to survive their forays into the Sprawl.

The store is run by Marla, a shrewd and resourceful woman who has lived in Blackvale her entire life. She sources goods from the remnants of the city and occasional outside traders who brave the curse's edge.

The shelves are stocked with essential items such as food rations, potions, torches, and other survival gear. Despite the scarcity of resources, Marla manages to keep the store well-supplied.

The General Store is crucial for players to resupply and prepare for their journeys into the Sprawl. Marla often has useful information about recent events and potential dangers in the city.

The Forge is a blacksmith's workshop located at the far end of Market Street. It is one of the few places where weapons and armor are still crafted and repaired.

The Forge is operated by Garin, a burly blacksmith with a gruff exterior but a heart of gold. Garin is a master of his craft, having apprenticed under his father and honed his skills over decades.

The Forge offers a variety of services, including weapon and armor crafting, repairs, and upgrades. Garin uses whatever materials he can salvage or trade for, ensuring that adventurers are well-equipped for their battles.

The Forge is vital for adventurers to maintain and enhance their equipment. Garin's craftsmanship can make the difference between life and death in the Sprawl. He also has stories of ancient weapons and lost techniques that may offer unique upgrades.

The training yard, once a prestigious training ground for Blackvale's elite warriors, has become a crucial sanctuary amid the city's curse. Its towering stone walls are adorned with the banners and insignias of long-forgotten champions, now tattered and faded. Despite the decay, the arena remains a place of discipline and skill, where those who dare to venture into the Sprawl can hone their abilities.

Surrounded by walls and guarded by ancient statues of past heroes, the training yard offers a stark contrast to the chaos beyond its gates. The ground is covered in soft sand, perfect for absorbing the impact of fierce combat training. Weapons and armor line the walls, and various training dummies and obstacles are scattered throughout the yard. The arena is divided into several sections: a sparring area, a skill practice zone, and a strategy planning corner.

Captain Seraphina Drake, a former commander of Blackvale's guard, now oversees the training yard. Known for her fierce determination and unwavering discipline, Seraphina was one of the few who resisted the curse's initial onslaught, using her skills and leadership to protect those she could. Her piercing green eyes reflect both her tactical brilliance and the sorrow of the city's downfall.

The Sprawl, once the thriving outskirts of Blackvale, now stands as a haunting reminder of the city's tragic fall. This vast area was originally a bustling district where merchants, craftsmen, and common folk lived and worked, contributing to the city's prosperity. Narrow streets, lined with quaint houses and vibrant markets, wove through the district, creating a lively and interconnected community.

However, with the arrival of Yelmurath's curse, the Sprawl became one of the first areas to succumb to the creeping darkness. Buildings that once echoed with laughter and trade now stand in ruin, their walls cracked and their windows shattered. The once-busy markets are now filled with the remains of abandoned stalls, and the cobblestone streets are overgrown with twisted, malevolent flora that seems to feed off the corruption in the air.

The Sprawl is now a dangerous and unpredictable place, inhabited by twisted creatures and haunted by restless spirits. The district is divided into several corrupted zones, each with its own unique horrors. Among these are the Catacombs, where the dead do not rest; the Blighted Gardens, overrun with monstrous plants; and the Forsaken Market, where cursed merchants continue their eternal transactions.

Despite its perilous nature, the Sprawl remains a crucial area for those seeking to understand the curse that plagues Blackvale. Brave souls venture into its depths, hoping to find clues, resources, or perhaps even a way to combat the dark magic that has taken hold. For these adventurers, the Sprawl offers both great danger and the promise of uncovering the secrets needed to save their city.

Beneath the city lies an ancient network of tunnels and crypts, known as the Catacombs of Blackvale. Originally intended as burial grounds for the city's honored dead, the catacombs have become a dark and treacherous place, overrun by the curse.
